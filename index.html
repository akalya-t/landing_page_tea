TEA

Tea is an undeniable part of daily life for billions across the world. More than just a beverage, it offers comfort, connection, and health benefits. In India, it's a symbol of hospitality and tradition, deeply woven into morning routines and social moments. Whether sipped alone or shared, tea is a daily ritual that energizes, soothes, and unites.


TYPES OF CHAI


MASALA CHAI

Black tea brewed with milk, sugar, and spices like cardamom, ginger, cloves, and cinnamon. Known for its warming, digestive, and stimulating effects.

GREEN CHAI

Especially popular in the Himalayan and northeastern regions. Known for detoxification and weight management.

HERBAL TEA (TISANES)

Include tulsi tea, ginger tea, lemongrass, ashwagandha, and others. Widely used in Ayurvedic medicine for various ailments.



MEDICINAL BENEFITS

RICH IN ANTIOXIDANTS


Especially catechins (in green tea) and theaflavins (in black tea), which help reduce oxidative stress and protect cells from damage.

BOOSTS HEART HEALTH


Regular tea consumption may help lower blood pressure, reduce LDL cholesterol, and improve blood vessel function.

SUPPORTS IMMUNE SYSTEM


Some teas, like tulsi (holy basil) or chamomile, have antimicrobial and adaptogenic properties that support immune function.

ENHANCES MENTAL ALERTNESS


Contains caffeine (though less than coffee) which can improve focus and concentration.



TEA IN AYURVEDA

In Ayurveda, different teas are used to balance the doshas (Vata, Pitta, Kapha).

Tea is often prescribed for:

  • Cleansing (detoxification)
  • Stress relief
  • Stress relief
  • Improving digestion
  • Increasing energy (prana)

FACTS


  • Tea is the second most consumed beverage in the world after water.
  • It’s estimated that over 2 billion people drink tea daily.
  • The global tea market was valued at over USD 200 billion in 2024 and is expected to grow steadily.
  • The top tea-consuming countries include China, India, Turkey, the UK, and Japan.
  • Indigenous tribes in Assam reportedly used tea leaves medicinally before British introduction.
